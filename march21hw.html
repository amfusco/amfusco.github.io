Women of Color in Congress

Below, you will find data illustrating representations of women of color in Congress. This data encompasses representation from the first woman of color to serve to the present Congress. Patsy Mink, a Democrat from Hawaii was the first women of color to be elected to Congress in 1965. In January 2019, 12 freshmen women of color were sworn into Congress bringing the total number of congresswomen of color in American history to 79.
